The World Of Tiffany

Q. Was it nicknamed the king of diamonds?

Our passion and foresight for the rarest and most exclusive jewels set the standard for American luxury. In 1837 he opened Tiffany & Co. as a fancy goods and stationery store in New York. In the 1840s, he bought jewelry from French nobles, continuing to redefine glamor and style. To commemorate the first appearance of a major gemstone in the United States, the press named me "King of Diamonds".

Q. What is the promise of craftsmanship?

Charles Tiffany was the first jeweler to establish the diamond ring as a symbol of love. In 1886 he introduced the Tiffany® Setting, the world’s most iconic engagement ring. Flawlessly engineered to allow the diamond to float above a delicate six-prong setting, it has played a part in the world’s greatest love stories for over 130 years. This innovation established Tiffany & Co. as more than a jewelry house—it has become a destination for timeless designs and unparalleled craftsmanship.

Attracting and nurturing diverse talents

Through its talent strategy, Tiffany seeks to reach a diverse workforce and expand other diversity and inclusion initiatives to serve its business, customers and communities. In 2020, we improved our talent recruitment process to add a performance zone of diversity and inclusion, and to remove the stereotypes that block people of color in our talent pipeline. In 2020, we also continued to add diversity and inclusion to the programs offered by our TiffanyU platform. Introducing an e-learning course on unconscious bias as part of new employee orientation and a practical program focused on how to alleviate unconscious bias has been an important area of ​​our work. Beginning in 2022, these sessions will be available in multiple languages.
